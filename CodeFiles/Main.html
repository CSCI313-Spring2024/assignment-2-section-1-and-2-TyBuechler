Introduction

My name is Ty Buechler, and I am a Junior at North Dakota State University (NDSU), majoring in Computer Science with a focus on Software Engineering and minoring in Artificial Intelligence. I have a strong passion for technology, problem-solving, and software development, and I’m particularly interested in areas like machine learning, cloud computing, and cybersecurity.

Outside of academics, I serve as the Vice President of Tau Kappa Epsilon (TKE) at NDSU. In this role, I help oversee chapter operations, recruitment, and event planning, while also fostering leadership and brotherhood within our fraternity. Being in a leadership position has helped me develop my skills in team management, organization, and strategic planning.

Professionally, I work in the IT Department at MasTec, a company based in Coral Gables, Florida. My role involves troubleshooting technical issues, assisting with system management, and gaining hands-on experience with networking, cybersecurity, and enterprise IT systems. This experience has given me valuable insight into the tech industry and how large organizations operate.

I grew up in Salisbury, North Carolina, where I lived for 15 years before moving to Brunswick, Georgia. From a young age, I’ve been fascinated by technology and how things work, which ultimately led me to pursue a career in software engineering.

About Me

Hi, my name is Ty Buechler! I'm a Junior at North Dakota State University, majoring in Computer Science with a focus on Software Engineering and minoring in Artificial Intelligence. My passion for technology and video games inspired me to pursue this field, as I love the idea of building innovative software that can improve lives.

My career interests are broad, ranging from cybersecurity to software development for major companies. Lately, I’ve been intrigued by the idea of enhancing sporting events with technology—things like ball-tracking systems for the NFL or other tools that could improve the fan and player experience.

Currently, I work in the IT Department at MasTec, where I developed a form system to track company assets and store device information, making it easier to handle repairs and maintenance. At NDSU, I’ve worked on projects like a dog adoption website for my CSCI 213 class and have even started building a website for my mom—though that one’s still on the back burner! My strongest programming language is Java, but I’m eager to expand my skills in Python and C#.

Outside of tech, I’m a huge Carolina Panthers fan and used to create YouTube videos doing Madden and College Football 25 rebuilds. I also love playing the drums, though having roommates has put a pause on that for now. Additionally, I’m the Vice President of Tau Kappa Epsilon, where I help organize service events, ensuring each member completes at least 10 service hours per semester.
